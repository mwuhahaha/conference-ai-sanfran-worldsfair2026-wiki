## Highlights
- Start by understanding where the customer is in its transformation journey before deciding to deploy FDE.
  - Evidence: "And the first thing is I want you to understand your customers or at least the target market that you're going after and where they are in their transformation journey."
  - Transcript: [[youtube-APqXGyCoGW4-transcript]]
- Do not hand FDE open-ended headcount; define the problem and the scope you intend to solve.
  - Evidence: "Does that measure success? Yes, great. Um I I have a lot of opinions about scope. So, I think that you cannot just do like, \"Hey, take two FTEs for 6 months, do whatever you want with them.\" I think that's a recipe for failure."

- Keep the customer involved in scoping, building, validation, and ROI review so the work does not drift away from their needs.
  - Evidence: "And so that would be my recommendation. Involve the customer in every step step. Scoping, actually building and designing the solution, implementation, doing the human in the loop validation, looking at baseline versus the results, identifying the ROI we're driving."

- Saying no to poor-fit use cases protects credibility and makes later partnership easier.
  - Evidence: "One is you build credibility by being very honest about where our applications and our products and our platform are the right tools and where they're not."
  - Transcript: [[youtube-APqXGyCoGW4-transcript]]

  - Evidence: "So, partner with your your uh system integrators and your consultants so they can go do that at scale and broaden in your reach."
