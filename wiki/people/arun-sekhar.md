# Arun Sekhar

## Profile
Arun Sekhar is Principal Product Manager for AI Developer Experience at [[microsoft|Microsoft]], an organization represented across 22 scheduled World’s Fair appearances by 14 affiliated speakers. His work centers on the decisions that move AI applications from prototype to production: selecting models and agent frameworks, designing developer tooling and application platforms, integrating [[mcp|Model Context Protocol]], and containing code-running agents inside secure cloud environments. His three World’s Fair sessions form a practical sequence—swap models in an existing application, choose among OpenAI, Anthropic, and agent-framework layers, then reduce OpenClaw’s operational blast radius with one-command cloud sandboxes.

## Biography
Arun Sekhar has worked as a developer, development lead, and product manager across [[microsoft|Microsoft]] technologies, giving him a perspective that spans implementation details, platform design, and product-level safeguards. At World’s Fair 2026, he joins Pamela Fox for [[2026-06-29-pamela-fox-the-model-swap-workshop|The model swap workshop]], a hands-on workshop about changing the model behind an AI application, and for [[2026-06-30-arun-sekhar-openai-anthropic-or-agent-frameworks-choose-the-right-ai-stack|OpenAI, Anthropic, or agent frameworks: choose the right AI stack]], which focuses on choosing the appropriate provider, framework, and abstraction layer. His third appearance, [[2026-07-01-arun-sekhar-blast-radius-zero-one-command-openclaw-sandboxes-in-the-cloud|Blast Radius Zero]], extends that stack-selection discussion into runtime operations by using one-command cloud isolation to constrain OpenClaw execution. Together, the sessions connect [[coding-agents|coding agents]] and [[mcp|Model Context Protocol]] integrations to the execution boundaries described by [[ai-sandboxes|AI sandboxes]] and the end-to-end controls emphasized in [[agent-security|agent security]].
