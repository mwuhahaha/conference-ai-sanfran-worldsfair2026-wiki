# Fuad Ali

## Profile
Fuad Ali is a Senior Product Manager at [[arize-ai|Arize AI]] focused on turning production evidence into practical improvements for deployed agents. His World’s Fair program spans two linked concerns: building self-learning systems that use traces, feedback, and [[agent-evaluations|agent evaluation]] to refine prompts, and making [[voice-agents|voice agents]] observable across speech recognition, reasoning, tool calls, and real-time responses. Rather than treating observability as passive monitoring, Ali presents it as the operating layer for finding behavioral failures, exposing missing feedback signals, and choosing the next prompt or system change.

No public profile links listed in the official speaker roster.

## Biography
Fuad Ali brings product and engineering experience from SpaceX, Tesla, Twitter, and Federato to his work on ML observability and reliable AI systems at Arize AI; he is also a co-host of The Next Iteration podcast. His two confirmed June 29 sessions form a concrete production-improvement loop. “Building self-learning loops for your agent” focuses on collecting feedback and evaluation evidence, diagnosing failure patterns, and repeatedly optimizing prompts. “Voice Agents Are Mostly Invisible. Here's How to See Them.” applies the same discipline to systems where recognition, reasoning, tool use, latency, and spoken responses unfold as a partially hidden sequence rather than a single inspectable output. The supporting Arize presentation “Build a Prompt Learning Loop,” delivered with SallyAnn DeLucia, supplies slide-derived examples around planning, prompt optimization, applied learning loops, case analysis, and gaps where useful feedback is missing. Across these sources, Ali’s contribution is a specific operational method: instrument the agent’s execution, evaluate traces and outcomes, identify weak or absent signals, and convert those findings into targeted prompt, workflow, or agent changes.
