# George Cameron

## Profile
George Cameron is Co-Founder of [[artificial-analysis|Artificial Analysis]], the independent benchmarking and market-analysis company whose public platform compares foundation models, AI agents, API hosts, inference performance, and hardware. At AI Engineer World’s Fair 2026, he joins Micah Hill Smith for the Autoresearch main-stage keynote “Trends in AI.” Cameron brings a market-wide measurement perspective to [[autoresearch|Autoresearch]]: keeping an objective fixed, gathering evidence, making a bounded change, measuring the result, and using that result to guide the next iteration.

## Biography
At [[artificial-analysis|Artificial Analysis]], George Cameron develops independent benchmarks, leaderboards, and evaluation frameworks covering AI agents, foundation models, inference providers, and hardware. That work supplies the comparative evidence needed to distinguish changes in model capability, provider performance, and deployment infrastructure from product positioning or market narratives. In the confirmed WF2026 keynote “Trends in AI,” Cameron and Micah Hill Smith connect this benchmarking perspective to [[autoresearch|Autoresearch]], presented on the Autoresearch main stage as a closed-loop engineering practice: retain a research objective, collect evidence, propose a bounded intervention, evaluate its outcome, and feed the measurement into the next cycle. Artificial Analysis’s work is regularly cited by frontier AI organizations including OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and NVIDIA.
