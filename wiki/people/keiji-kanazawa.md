# Keiji Kanazawa

## Profile
Keiji Kanazawa is a Principal Product Manager for AI inference in [[microsoft|Microsoft]] Foundry, working with Anthropic and OpenAI models where model capability meets production operation. With a Ph.D. in AI and experience spanning research, engineering, and product management, he focuses on the runtimes, controls, and evaluation practices needed to keep long-running agent systems dependable and inspectable. His two confirmed World’s Fair sessions address that lifecycle from opposite ends. With Tina Manghnani, he follows an agent from framework-based development into Microsoft Foundry Agent Service, where managed execution introduces concrete responsibilities around deployment, isolation, and observability. In his solo AI Architects: Tokenmaxxing session, he examines the repository-level consequences of letting [[coding-agents|coding agents]] refactor a codebase autonomously for three weeks, then reviews the generated code and architectural decisions rather than accepting task completion as proof of quality. Together, the sessions treat runtime governance, code review, evaluation evidence, and trust as one continuous production discipline.

## Biography
Kanazawa (@gojira) approaches agents as production software whose execution, generated artifacts, safety boundaries, and evaluation trail must remain open to inspection. “From framework to runtime” centers on the handoff from a locally assembled agent to Microsoft Foundry Agent Service: not merely changing where the agent runs, but moving it into a service-backed environment responsible for sustained execution and operational control. “I Let Agents Refactor My Codebase for 3 Weeks. Then I Read the Code” tests the complementary question of what remains after extended autonomy. By auditing the agent’s repository changes, architectural choices, and tradeoffs, the session makes maintainability and human review part of the evaluation rather than treating a completed refactor as the endpoint. A separate public AI Engineer recording with Nagkumar Arkalgud extends this production-systems perspective through Microsoft’s AI Red Teaming Agent for Azure AI Foundry. Its ten slide-derived signals emphasize accelerated red-team workflows, safety, security, and controlled execution, connecting Kanazawa’s evaluation concerns to the containment model described by [[ai-sandboxes|AI sandboxes]]. That video and its extracted, dense, and reconstructed slide decks provide supporting context only; they are not confirmed official World’s Fair 2026 media. Kanazawa is also an angel investor in YC and AI/ML companies.
