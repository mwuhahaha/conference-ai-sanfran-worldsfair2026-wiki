# Laurie Voss

## Profile
Laurie Voss is Head of Developer Relations at [[arize-ai|Arize AI]], where he focuses on the evaluation and observability practices needed to move agents from convincing demos into dependable production systems. His five confirmed World’s Fair appearances connect that work across two hands-on [[agent-evaluations|agent evaluation]] workshops, the Evals track introduction, a session on how [[coding-agents|coding agents]] reshape code review, and a [[mcp|context-engineering]] talk about how instruction length affects an agent’s ability to retain and apply skills. Together, the sessions position evaluation as part of the full engineering loop: tracing behavior, constructing datasets, judging outputs, reviewing agent-produced changes, and controlling the context available at inference time.

## Biography
Voss has worked as a developer for more than 30 years and co-founded npm, Inc., giving him direct experience with infrastructure shifts that change how software is built, distributed, and reviewed. At [[arize-ai|Arize AI]], he brings that perspective to AI observability and [[agent-evaluations|agent evaluation]], including production traces, representative datasets, automated and human judges, output-quality analysis, and the question of whether an agent remains reliable beyond a successful demonstration. The linked “Ship Real Agents” transcript and slide material reinforces this practical emphasis through detailed treatment of evals, tracing, datasets, judges, and Phoenix, while remaining supporting context rather than a verified World’s Fair 2026 recording. His confirmed program extends the same production discipline into [[autoresearch|autoresearch]], agent-driven code-review workflows, and context limits: how much instruction a skill can carry before an agent forgets or fails to apply it. This connects his conference role to the broader concerns of [[coding-agents|coding agents]], [[mcp|Model Context Protocol]], and [[inference-engineering|inference engineering]]—measuring behavior, supplying usable context, and making model capability dependable inside real systems.
