# Sandhya Subramani

## Profile
Sandhya Subramani is a Senior Developer Advocate for generative AI at [[amazon-web-services|Amazon Web Services]], focused on turning large language models and agentic systems into practical engineering workflows. Her AI Engineer World’s Fair 2026 program connects [[coding-agents|coding agents]], dynamic tool creation, and enterprise intent: she co-leads a workshop that follows an idea through coding, deployment, observation, repair, and release, then presents sessions on agents that forge their own tools and on communicating organizational goals clearly enough for AI systems to act on them.

- [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sandhyasubramani/)

## Biography
Subramani brings more than eight years of applied AI research and engineering experience across Amazon, Warner Bros, and Fidelity Investments. At the conference, that background appears in three distinct but related settings: the Agent Speedrun workshop treats coding agents as participants in an operational software lifecycle; “Agents That Forge Their Own Tools” examines systems that adapt by creating capabilities at runtime; and “Tell the Robot What You Want” moves the problem into the enterprise, where effective agent behavior depends on translating human and organizational intent into actionable direction. Together, the sessions frame agent engineering as more than model prompting: it requires repository-aware development, observable feedback loops, controlled tool access—including shared capability layers such as [[mcp|Model Context Protocol]]—and clear definitions of what an autonomous system should accomplish.
