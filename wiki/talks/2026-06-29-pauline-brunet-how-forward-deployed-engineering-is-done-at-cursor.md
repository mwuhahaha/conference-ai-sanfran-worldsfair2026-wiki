## Synthesis
### Transcript-Backed Summary
Pauline Brunet argues that forward deployed engineering only works when you match the motion to both customer maturity and product customizability. Her core mechanism is a project-based, highly technical, customer-facing engagement model that co-designs solutions with named stakeholders, scopes the work around concrete business outcomes, and keeps the customer involved through implementation and validation. The main tradeoff is that FDE can drive adoption, surface product feedback, and expand what the platform can do, but it becomes wasteful if it degenerates into training, documentation, or staff augmentation. The practical consequence is that companies need a clear mission, strict scope, and explicit ROI criteria before they hire or deploy the function.

### Key Takeaways
- Start by understanding where the customer is in its transformation journey before deciding to deploy FDE.
  - Evidence: "And the first thing is I want you to understand your customers or at least the target market that you're going after and where they are in their transformation journey."
- Do not hand FDE open-ended headcount; define the problem and the scope you intend to solve.
  - Evidence: "Does that measure success? Yes, great. Um I I have a lot of opinions about scope. So, I think that you cannot just do like, \"Hey, take two FTEs for 6 months, do whatever you want with them.\" I think that's a recipe for failure."
- Keep the customer involved in scoping, building, validation, and ROI review so the work does not drift away from their needs.
  - Evidence: "And so that would be my recommendation. Involve the customer in every step step. Scoping, actually building and designing the solution, implementation, doing the human in the loop validation, looking at baseline versus the results, identifying the ROI we're driving."
- Saying no to poor-fit use cases protects credibility and makes later partnership easier.
  - Evidence: "One is you build credibility by being very honest about where our applications and our products and our platform are the right tools and where they're not."
- Use system integrators and consultants when you need to broaden reach or scale rollout work.
  - Evidence: "So, partner with your your uh system integrators and your consultants so they can go do that at scale and broaden in your reach."

### Claims From The Talk
- For mature customers with low product customization, the speaker says self-service onboarding and documentation are a better fit than FDE. (`explicit`)
  - Evidence: "So, if you have a customer who is really mature in their digital transformation, they have engineers, um it's a low customization on your project, I would really just say provide your product in a self-service fashion, create give great documentation, probably not a great use of your FDE motion."
- For mature customers with highly customizable products, the speaker says FDE should act more as advisers that help accelerate adoption. (`explicit`)
  - Evidence: "Here, you're going to have crazy adoption, right? And I would say a lot of it should be your FDE team should be acting as advisers and help accelerate them."
- For customers that are earlier in their transformation journey, the speaker frames FDE as embedded transformation support. (`explicit`)
  - Evidence: "They're learning a little bit about how to do it. They may not be able to hire or staff the folks that you you And so, they need a lot more help and here is where I think of like that embedded transformation."
- At Cursor, FDE work is project-based and is typically scoped with an economic buyer or senior champion around a strategic objective. (`explicit`)
  - Evidence: "So, for us, it is project-based, highly impactful projects. So, I usually would like to work with the economic buyer or a very senior champion within my accounts to make sure that we are scoping something that is a strategic objective for the company, right?"
- The speaker says FDE is the tip of the spear for pushing edge cases and testing new use cases with customers. (`explicit`)
  - Evidence: "So, as we get really cool use cases that are a little bit outside of the software development life cycle, the FTE team to me is the tip of the spear that is going to test out these new use cases with customers."
- The speaker warns that co-developing in a customer code base can drift into staff augmentation unless the work is clearly collaborative. (`explicit`)
  - Evidence: "We work in co-development with customer teams in their code base. This is where you have to be really careful that you end up uh not doing staff augmentation."
- The speaker reduces FDE success to three business outcomes: increasing revenue, decreasing costs, or mitigating risk. (`explicit`)
  - Evidence: "It's always three things, super simple. Am I increasing revenue? Am I decreasing costs? Or am I mitigating risks?"

### Topics Covered
- [[coding-agents|FDE fit matrix]] — Choosing whether FDE fits by comparing customer maturity with product configurability.
- [[coding-agents|Project-based delivery]] — Using FDE as a project-based motion tied to strategic objectives and named sponsors.
- [[coding-agents|Embedded co-development]] — Working directly with customer teams inside their code base instead of operating as a detached service layer.
- **ROI framing** — Judging success by revenue, cost, or risk impact rather than by activity volume.
- **Product feedback loop** — Turning close customer work into signals for product and roadmap decisions.

### Tools And Named Systems

- **Cursor SDK** — The developer kit used for building applications on top of Cursor.

### Novel Concepts And Methods
- **Fit matrix** — Place accounts on a customer-maturity versus product-customizability matrix before deciding whether FDE is the right motion.
- **Directional scoping** — Scope engagements directionally around a specific problem, KPI, and phased deliverable instead of open-ended headcount.
- **Customer-in-the-loop delivery** — Keep the customer involved from scoping through implementation, validation, and ROI review.
- **Partner-led scaling** — Use system integrators and consultants to scale rollout and broaden reach when the work does not need to stay inside the FDE team.

### Open Questions
- **How should an FDE organization split technical depth from customer-facing skill as it grows?** — The speaker says the role will eventually split, but the transition path and hiring boundary are still open.
- **When should FDE coverage move from geography-based organization to industry-based specialization?** — The speaker says industry language matters for credibility, but does not define the operating trigger for that shift.
- **How should teams measure the true ROI of expensive automation when the direct operating cost looks high?** — The speaker gives an example where daily agent cost seemed high until the downstream savings were framed correctly.
