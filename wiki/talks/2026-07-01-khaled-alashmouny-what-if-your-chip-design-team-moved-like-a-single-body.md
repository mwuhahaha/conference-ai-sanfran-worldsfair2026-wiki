# What If Your Chip Design Team Moved Like a Single Body?

## Summary
This AI Architects session treats chip design as a long-horizon coordination system, not a place where a faster single-user copilot is enough. Khaled Alashmouny, founder and CEO of AIDAChip, and Abduallah Mohamed, AIDAChip's VP of AI/ML and former Meta practitioner, use semiconductor IP development to make the case for Multiplayer AI: many specialized agents working across disciplines while staying anchored to the same intent, methodology, and project milestones. The talk's central claim is that the expensive failure mode in chip teams is silent divergence: specs drift, disciplines make incompatible assumptions, and handoffs become costly because alignment infrastructure is missing.

The concrete architecture described here centers on three alignment layers for semiconductor engineering teams: a living spec graph or System of Intent that propagates changes and detects conflicts, a memory layer that captures methodology and tribal knowledge across projects, and milestone-aware execution that can drive EDA workflows with full design context. Alashmouny brings the founder/operator frame from AIDAChip's push toward multiplayer AI for engineering organizations, while Mohamed adds the AI platform perspective around agentic AI, LLM frameworks, multimodal systems, and production ML practice. Together they position chip design as a stress test for agent systems because the work spans many agents, many disciplines, strict spec hierarchies, audit requirements, and 12-month project arcs where a single missed assumption can be enormously expensive.

## Official Description
Most agentic demos you've seen has a hidden assumption: one user, one session, one task. But what

happens when the agent needs to coordinate with 30 other agents, across 10 disciplines, on a project

that takes 12 months — where a single miscommunication costs $10-50M? Chip design is that problem.

Only 14% of chips succeed on first silicon. The bottleneck isn't individual engineer speed — it's

silent divergence between disciplines working from specs that drift without noticing. We built a

multiplayer AI on the Anthropic Agent SDK, connected through three alignment layers: a living spec

graph (System of Intent) that propagates changes and detects conflicts in real time, a tribal

knowledge layer (Memory) that compounds methodology across projects, and milestone-aware execution

that drives EDA tools with full design context. Each agent operates within strict spec-hierarchy

boundaries enforced at the API level. Cross-agent invocations use structured tool calls with typed

parameters, logged for full auditability. We talked with 15 practitioners across 8 major

semiconductor and EDA companies. The universal finding: teams need alignment infrastructure, not

faster copilots. We'll also share what broke — because coordination tax applies to AI agents too,

and the failure modes are surprisingly instructive. This talk covers the multi-agent architecture,

evaluation methodology, and lessons from deploying agentic AI in one of engineering's most complex

coordination domains.
